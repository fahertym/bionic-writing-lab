The lab holds essays, books, pamphlets, fiction, poems, posts, notes, and experiments. It is built as a working archive rather than a feed: different forms keep their own shape, and the source remains plain text.

The recurring subject is machinery: systems, power, institutions, political machinery, technology, injury, survival, and the human consequences of structures people are taught to treat as natural.

Bionic Writing Lab is the explanatory side of that work: a place where fragments, drafts, concepts, arguments, essays, pamphlets, and books can accumulate without becoming disposable feed material.

Related Infrastructure Work

Matt also works on democratic infrastructure through the InterCooperative Network. That work is adjacent to this lab: ICN is institutional, governance, and economic coordination infrastructure; Bionic Writing Lab is cognitive, publishing, and explanatory infrastructure.

The lab can hold the systems writing that makes ICN legible without turning every page into an ICN advertisement.
