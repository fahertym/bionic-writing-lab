Public archive and publishing lab

Writing about the machinery underneath things.

A publishing lab for arguments that outgrew the feed. The lab converts fragments, drafts, arguments, essays, books, fiction, and AI-assisted notes into structured public artifacts.

Not a feed

Fragments should not die in the feed. Drafts, notes, essays, pamphlets, books, poems, and fiction can mature without being flattened into one chronological stream.

Forms stay distinct

Form matters. A poem is not a post. A pamphlet is not a chapter by force. A concept is not a tag.

Source and surface

Source is durable; surfaces are generated. Markdown and manifests produce site pages, downloads, feeds, search, concepts, paths, and relationship views.
