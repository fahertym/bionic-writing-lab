# Content Map

Bionic Writing Lab now has a first real intellectual skeleton.

This is not a content dump. It is a map for imports, revisions, reading paths, concepts, and relationships so the archive grows with structure from the start.

## First Concept Clusters

The first real concept batch establishes the recurring machinery of the work:

- `survival-pressure`: coercion through material penalty and dependency.
- `coercion`: force, threat, dependency, and constrained access.
- `property-as-command`: ownership as authority over access, labor, shelter, and survival.
- `capitalism-as-cancer`: runaway extraction and growth logic as system pathology.
- `religion-as-infrastructure`: mythology becoming law, hierarchy, punishment, obedience, or state power.
- `artificial-scarcity`: engineered scarcity used as leverage.
- `institutional-laundering`: harm and domination converted into procedure, legitimacy, or neutral administration.
- `legitimacy`: the permission structure that makes power appear rightful or natural.
- `democratic-infrastructure`: tools and institutions for coordination without default surrender to owners, bosses, platforms, or states.
- `ai-as-amplifier`: AI as a force multiplier for attention, drafting, compression, pattern recognition, and system-building.

These concepts are draft scaffolds until real publications are attached. Do not attach fake publication IDs. Empty `publications` arrays are allowed for draft concept work.

## Maturation Pipeline

The lab should preserve early forms instead of forcing them into finished containers.

```text
fragment -> post -> essay -> pamphlet -> chapter/book
```

This is not a mandatory ladder. Some fragments will stay fragments. Some essays will never become pamphlets. Some books may produce excerpts, posts, or concepts in reverse.

Use publication relationships to record movement:

- `expands`: a later work develops an earlier seed.
- `responds_to`: a piece answers another piece or argument.
- `supersedes`: a newer version replaces an older one.
- `related`: a looser connection.
- `excerpt_of`: a short piece comes from a longer work.
- `adapted_from`: a piece is reshaped from earlier material.

## Import Assignment Rules

During import, assign concepts and relationships slowly.

For each imported item, ask:

- What kind is it really: fragment, post, essay, pamphlet, book, poem, series, or collection?
- Is it `imported`, `draft`, `review`, or ready for `published`?
- Which concept IDs are explicit enough to attach now?
- Does it expand, adapt, excerpt, respond to, supersede, or relate to an existing publication?
- Does it belong in a reading path now, or should it wait?

Do not infer every possible concept. Concept links should be intentional and maintainable.

## ICN Position

ICN is a future major cluster, not the whole lab.

Bionic Writing Lab is cognitive, publishing, and explanatory infrastructure. ICN is institutional, governance, and economic coordination infrastructure.

ICN-related fragments may eventually include governance notes, civic interface sketches, cooperative economics, protocol arguments, public explanations, and pamphlet drafts. Those should use the same concept, path, and relationship machinery as everything else.

Do not hard-code ICN as the center of the archive. Let it become a major reading path and concept cluster when the content earns that structure.

## Anti-Dump Rule

Imports should arrive through a loading dock, not a dump truck.

Do not import a major archive and fix the metadata later. Start with a few representative pieces, validate the model, then expand.

Every import should preserve source, choose a kind, set lifecycle status, avoid fake references, and leave a clear path for later concept/path/relationship assignment.
