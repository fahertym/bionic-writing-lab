# God's Name On The Letterhead

Here's the test. If you found this document in an archaeological dig, no title page, no attribution, no tradition attached, and you read the slavery provisions, the rape pricing, the virginity inspections, the execution schedule, and the foreskin payments, you would classify it immediately. Ancient Near Eastern legal code. Patriarchal. Hierarchical. Violent. Typical of its era.

You wouldn't call it the word of God. You'd call it the word of men who needed god's name on the letterhead.

The signature doesn't change the content. The content tells you who actually wrote it.

If God's law is merely progressive for 1200 BCE, then it's a human document reflecting human progress, not divine legislation transcending time. The defense accidentally concedes the argument: God's morality improved alongside human morality, which means human morality was doing the improving.
